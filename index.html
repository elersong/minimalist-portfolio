The Hack.Summit( )

Winter 2014

I served as an operational member of the 9-person team that organized and hosted a technology conference around the idea of mentorship that went on to break the record for the world's largest tech conference by number of registrations. I wrote spiders in ruby to collect and process data managed in a PostGRES database through a Django interface.

The Bibble

Fall 2020

As an exercise in language processing and creative writing, I started "The Bibble," an attempt to bridge the gap between religious text and secular morals. What would the Bible look like if it weren't written for a religious audience? I designed an interface that would organize a secular interpretation beside original biblical text, and I plan to build the application using Aurelia, React and Firebase.

The Plotline

Spring 2020

I built The Plotline using ruby Sinatra, the Twilio phone system API and the Stripe payment system API to create a telephone-based private broadcast system for delivery of short stories written by small-renown authors. For a small fee, users could hear a recording of a short story performance, complete with sound effects. The proceeds would cover production costs and support small writers.

Who Am I?

I'm the best hiring decision you'll make this year. I not only pay close attention to the needs of my teammates, but I seek to seamlessly integrate into my work environments, boosting efficiency and productivity through simple solutions that leverage existing resources. This drive to seek continued growth both personally and for my peers tends to leave me unsatisfied, but it also results in strong connections with those around me and thorough explorations of project goals.

I've worked for years in the education sector, focusing heavily on technology and engineering. I have a strong network of mentors/advisors and a large collection of my own students, who have gone one to accomplish great things. I enjoy working with teams and discovering new degrees of flexibility I was not previously aware I possessed.

From business strategy and product design to client account management and customer support, I have also built years of management experience working on teams to craft processes that consistently meet key project performance goals. Not only am I comfortable speaking before groups, I actively enjoy the opportunity to cultivate "eureka!" moments among my peers.

My professional goals between now and 2025 focus on identifying teams that push themselves to consistently and objectively improve, altering business practice to meet new needs as they arise rather than altering perception of needs to meet comfortable and pre-determined business practices. I like to focus only upon what's necessary and remove the extraneous.

If my goals align with those of your team, and you'd like to get in touch to discuss opportunities to collaborate, feel free to reach out to me via email at grey.l.elerson@gmail.com or by submitting a message in the contact form below. For more explanation of my work experience, please see my Github and LinkedIn profiles.
